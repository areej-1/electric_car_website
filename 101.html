  • Electric Cars 101

Electric cars (EVs) are vehicles powered by electricity instead of gasoline. They use a battery pack to store energy and an electric motor to move the car, replacing the traditional engine. When the driver presses the accelerator, the battery sends electricity through a controller/inverter, which delivers the right amount of power to the motor and turns the wheels.


EVs are known for being quiet, efficient, and environmentally friendly, since they produce zero exhaust emissions while driving. They can be recharged by plugging into a charger, and many electric cars also use regenerative braking, which helps recover energy when slowing down and sends it back into the battery.


Overall, electric cars are an important part of the future of transportation, combining technology, sustainability, and performance in one system.
