Electric Vehicles 101

Master the fundamentals of electric vehicle technology, from battery chemistry to regenerative braking systems.

Battery Technology

The battery pack is the heart of every EV. Most modern electric vehicles use lithium-ion batteries due to their high energy density and long cycle life.

  • Energy Density: 150-265 Wh/kg
  • Cycle Life: 1,000-2,000 charge cycles
  • Voltage: 400V-800V systems
Formula E cars use 54 kWh battery packs capable of delivering up to 350 kW of power.

Electric Motors

EVs use electric motors that convert electrical energy into mechanical motion. The main types are:

  • AC Induction: Robust, used by Tesla Model S
  • Permanent Magnet: Higher efficiency, most common
  • Switched Reluctance: Simple, no magnets needed
Electric motors deliver instant torque from 0 RPM, enabling rapid acceleration without gear changes.

Power Electronics

The inverter and controller manage power flow between the battery and motor, converting DC to AC and controlling speed.

  • Inverter: Converts DC battery power to AC
  • Controller: Manages power delivery
  • BMS: Battery Management System monitors cell health
Modern inverters use silicon carbide (SiC) semiconductors for 5-10% efficiency gains.

Regenerative Braking

When decelerating, the motor runs in reverse as a generator, converting kinetic energy back into electrical energy.

  • Energy Recovery: Up to 70% of braking energy
  • Range Extension: 10-25% more range
  • Brake Wear: Significantly reduced
One-pedal driving is possible because regen braking can fully stop the car without touching the brake pedal.

Charging Systems

EVs can be charged at different power levels depending on the infrastructure:

  • Level 1: 120V AC, ~5 km/hour
  • Level 2: 240V AC, ~40 km/hour
  • DC Fast: 50-350 kW, ~500 km/hour
800V architecture (Porsche Taycan, Hyundai Ioniq 5) enables 10-80% charge in under 18 minutes.

Efficiency & Range

Electric vehicles are significantly more efficient than internal combustion engines:

  • EV Efficiency: 85-90% (battery to wheel)
  • ICE Efficiency: 20-30% (fuel to wheel)
  • Cost: ~$0.04/km vs $0.12/km for gas
The Tesla Model S Long Range achieves over 650 km on a single charge with a 100 kWh battery.
